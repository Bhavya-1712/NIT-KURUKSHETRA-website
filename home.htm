In 1963, under the government of India, the National Institute of Technology Kurukshetra (NIT Kurukshetra) was created. The institute initially began operating as a Regional Engineering College (REC), but on June 26, 2002, it later obtained the status of a Deemed University. Since it received the status of a Deemed University, the institute was later renamed NIT Kurukshetra. NIT Kurukshetra conducts undergraduate and postgraduate education in different disciplines. The college offers Ph.D doctoral degrees in engineering, technology, humanities, liberal arts and graphical interfaces. NIT Kurukshetra's mission is to provide quality technical education that produces creative practitioners and entrepreneurs and to pursue research-based on socio-economic needs that creates cutting-edge technology and futuristic expertise. The National Institute of Technology Kurukshetra campus stretches over an area of 292 acres on a picturesque setting imaginatively laid down. It offers a spectacle of artistic order and natural elegance.
